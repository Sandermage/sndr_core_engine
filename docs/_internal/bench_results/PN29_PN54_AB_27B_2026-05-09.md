# PN29 + PN54 GDN Tweaks — 4-Arm Sweep on 27B PROD (2026-05-09)

## Setup

- Model: Qwen3.6-27B-int4-AutoRound + TQ k8v4 + MTP K=3 + 131K ctx (PROD)
- 2× A5000, vllm dev93, current 27B PROD baseline (P82=1 thr=0.1 promoted)
- Bench: `genesis_bench_suite.py --quick` (5×5×1024)

## Verdict

**NULL impact across all 4 arms.** TPS variance ≤ 0.32%, well within
single-bench CV (~3-4%). Tool-call quality preserved (7/7) at every
combination.

## Hypothesis

Both PN29 (GDN scale fold) and PN54 (GDN contiguous dedup) target
specific GDN allocator hot paths that the **current** 27B PROD
config (TQ k8v4 + chunked prefill + MTP K=3) doesn't traverse
heavily. They may only matter on:

- Different `max_num_seqs` (current=4); higher batch may amplify the
  GDN allocator pressure these patches address.
- Different KV dtype (e.g. fp8_e5m2 instead of TQ k8v4); the
  contiguity assumptions may differ.
- Spec-decode OFF runs (the GDN scale fold may not interact with
  the K+1 verify path).

## Decision

**Both stay default OFF.** No reason to enable on current 27B PROD
config — no measurable TPS or quality gain. Re-test if the workload
shifts to higher `max_num_seqs` or a different KV dtype.
