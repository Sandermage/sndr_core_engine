# PN12 FFN Intermediate Scratch Pool — A/B on 35B PROD (2026-05-09)

## Setup

- Model: Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-FP8 + TQ k8v4 + MTP K=3 + 320K ctx (PROD)
- 2× A5000, vllm dev93, all Wave 7 + Sprint 1 patches enabled
- Bench: `genesis_bench_suite.py --quick` (5×5×1024)

## Results

| Arm | PN12 | wall_TPS | CV     | TPOT_ms | GPU0 MiB | GPU1 MiB | Δ TPS | Δ VRAM |
|-----|------|----------|--------|---------|----------|----------|-------|--------|
| A   | 0    | 233.92   | 0.0571 | 3.96    | 21954    | 21954    | base  | base   |
| B   | 1    | 234.25   | 0.0711 | 3.94    | 21954    | 21954    | +0.14% | 0 MiB  |

## Verdict

**NULL impact on 35B FP8 PROD config.** Expected -1.7 GiB VRAM savings
NOT observed (VRAM byte-identical between arms). TPS +0.14% within
noise (single-run CV ~6%).

## Hypothesis

PN12 (FFN intermediate scratch pool) targets a specific MoE allocator
pattern that 35B-A3B's Marlin tier doesn't trigger. The patch may only
provide measurable benefit on:

- INT4 quantization (vs the FP8 in this bench)
- Different `intermediate_size` shapes
- A different gate-MLP fusion path

## Decision

**Keep PN12 default OFF on 35B PROD YAML.** No reason to enable since
no measurable benefit. Re-test on 27B INT4 if the 27B test rig is
available (where the FFN pool semantics may align).
